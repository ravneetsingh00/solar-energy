INTRODUCTION OF SOLAR ENERGY

How Solar Panels Work:

  • Absorption of Sunlight: Solar panels are made up of photovoltaic (PV) cells that absorb sunlight.
  • Photovoltaic Effect: When sunlight hits the PV cells, it excites electrons in the semiconductor material (usually silicon), causing them to move.
  • Electric Current Generation: The movement of these electrons generates an electric current, which is captured by the panelâ€™s wiring.
  • Direct Current (DC): The generated electricity is in the form of direct current (DC).
  • Inverter: The DC electricity is then converted into alternating current (AC) by an inverter, which is the type of electricity used in homes and businesses.
  • Powering Devices: The AC electricity is supplied to electrical appliances and systems, or it can be stored in batteries for later use.
